PTL Kapture Tickets | 20 Feb - 19 Mar 2026 | Kapture + Metabase Ping Data

How Recharge Commissions Work — Understanding the Flow

When a partner recharges a customer, the ISP ticket (which carries the commission) becomes visible at midnight (00:00) the next day. If the customer’s device is offline (no pings), the recharge cannot process through the device — meaning no ISP ticket is generated, and the partner sees no commission. This is the root cause we are investigating.

Partners call PTL complaining about missing commissions. The question is: was the device even online when the recharge was supposed to happen?

Same pattern: recharge at 14:50 → ISP ticket starts at midnight. The commission is tied to the ISP ticket, not the recharge time. If the device has no ping at recharge time, the transaction fails silently.

This report checks: For every recharge commission complaint call, was the customer’s device actually pinging (online) at the time of the call? If not, the device was offline — the customer’s internet was down — and the recharge could not have processed. The partner’s complaint is about a symptom (no commission), but the root cause is device connectivity failure.
